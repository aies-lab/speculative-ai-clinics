Overview

Project in proposal and development stage; dates to be announced.

Grounded Speculative AI Clinics are student-run, community-partnered clinics that support local organizations navigating interest or pressure to adopt AI tools. Students learn to treat speculation as a structured way to anticipate harms, clarify responsibilities, and define thresholds for repair, safeguards, pause, refusal, or decommission before tools are adopted or workflows are reshaped.

Clinic teams partner with libraries, museums, archives, cultural organizations, and human and social services nonprofits. Projects are bounded and feasible, prioritizing risk reduction and strengthening existing systems rather than building high-risk AI deployments.

Timeline: Cohort 1 runs Autumn 2026–Spring 2027; Cohort 2 runs Autumn 2027–Spring 2028.

Primary audiences

  • Undergraduate students across computing, design, humanities, and policy.
  • Community organizations in the Puget Sound region.
  • RCC community members seeking reusable responsible computing materials.

How to Engage

Students

Join a clinic team or observe a Public Futures Review Board session to learn how speculative accountability works in practice, build applied ethics experience, and contribute to portfolio-ready governance artifacts.

Civic Organizations

Partner with students to speculate and dream about your organization’s future, surface accountability questions, and define responsible options together.

What you'll receive: decision-ready recommendations, a Speculative Accountability Packet, and partner-ready materials you can use internally.

Responsible Computing Community

Connect with educators, practitioners, and researchers to share modules, adapt clinic materials, and co-develop responsible computing practices, toolkits, and case studies that can travel across campuses.

You are also invited to observe or participate in Public Futures Review Board sessions.

Students

Turn your INFO 35X work into a longer project with community impact. The clinics connect the Information and Society series—INFO 351, 352, 353, and 354—to applied, team-based projects with partners, giving you a clear pathway from classroom inquiry to accountable practice.

Apply to join a clinic team, take on a leadership role, or observe a Public Futures Review Board session. You will produce portfolio-ready governance artifacts and practice decision-making in real institutional contexts. You'll co-scope a project with a community partner, map a workflow and identify adoption decision points, and deliver a Speculative Accountability Packet with recommendations.

What students learn

  • Analyze AI systems as sociotechnical systems with real stakeholders and power.
  • Evaluate tools using accountable practices: privacy, accessibility, safety, and validity.
  • Produce decision-ready governance artifacts and go/no-go thresholds.
  • Facilitate structured stakeholder critique via a Public Futures Review Board.

Partner Collaboration

Projects are co-defined with community organizations and include structured check-ins, review board milestones, and partner-approved outputs. The clinics prioritize reciprocity and decision support over automation.

What partners receive

Decision-ready recommendations, a Speculative Accountability Packet, and materials teams can use internally to guide adoption, repair, pause, or refusal decisions.

Typical engagement: 1–2 meetings per term plus a Public Futures Review Board session.

Interested in partnering or learning more? Reach out to the project team to discuss fit and timing for upcoming clinic cycles.

Clinic Model

Speculative Accountability Clinics

Students and partners co-scope projects, map workflows, and surface decision points driving AI adoption. Deliverables focus on safer alternatives, evaluation plans, and concrete improvements in privacy, accessibility, or process design.

Public Futures Review Board

A student-led review board convenes partners and stakeholders at milestones, requires revisions, documents tradeoffs, and recommends proceed, repair, pause, refuse, or decommission.

Speculative Accountability Packet

Each team produces a packet of scenarios, harm pathways, counterfactual redesigns, decision thresholds, and an option menu to support accountable choices.
